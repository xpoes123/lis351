Math

These are some math problems that I've found online that I have solved recently and I felt are cool enough to share. Answers are all listed at the end.

  • Combinatorics

    1. In Pascal's Triangle, each entry is the sum of the two entries above it. In which row of Pascal's Triangle do three consecutive entires occur that are in the ratio 3:4:5? (Source: AIME 1992 Problem 4)
    2. In how many ways can you mark 8 of the 16 cells in a 4 x 4 grid of cells so that there are 2 marked cells in each row, 2 marked cells in each column, and neither of the diagonals have all 4 of their cells marked? (Source: UWMTS 2022 5 question 1)
    3. The 20 members of a local tennis club have scheduled exactly 14 two-person games among themselves, with each member playing in at least one game. Prove that within this schedule there must be a set of 6 games with 12 distinct players (Source: USAMO 1989 Problem 2)

  • Number Theory

    1. Show that 1 to the n + 2 to the n + ... + n to the n is divisible by n squared given that n is an odd integer. (Source: UW Undergraduate Math Competition 2019 Problem 3)
    2. What is the smallest prime factor of the fraction with numerator 2022 to the 2022 + 6 and denominator 6? (Source: Michael Penn)
    3. Find all pairs of nonnegative integers (m,n) such that (m + n - 5) squared = 9mn. (Source: USATST 2001 Problem 8)
